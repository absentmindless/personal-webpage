ESE 319 Final Project

Metal detector.

Final project for analog circuits/amplifiers course. Designed three-stage amplifier in Altium, simulated using LTSpice. The circuit works by comparing the frequencies of two waves whose frequencies are set by separate LC oscillators; one oscillator uses a fixed inductor, while the other uses a variable inductor whose inductance changes in the presence of metal. The difference between the frequencies of these oscillators is isolated and then amplified through a speaker; as the variable inductor approaches a metallic object, its inductance changes, which leads to a change in the sound played by the speaker.

Board Games

I'm a big fan of abstract strategy games, and have prototyped a few of my favorites to share this passion with friends.

Quarto (top right) is essentially a four-dimensional tic-tac-toe played on a two-dimensional board. In an added twist, your opponent selects the pieces that you use on your turn. Play against a computer here.

Pentago (top left) is also similar to tic-tac-toe, but it's played on an expanded board and requires rows of five as a win condition. On each turn, you must twist one of the "sub"boards. Play it here--you'll need to play for both players.

Yinsh (bottom) is one of the seven games in the award-winning Gipf series by Kris Burm, and is much more involved. Designing the pieces for the gameboard was an interesting challenge. Read more about the game here

I'm currently working on a set for Chinese chess. Stay tuned!
