Senior Design Project (ongoing)

Wearable device to diagnose opioid overdose and administer treatment.

Electrical: Using embedded software (C, Assembly) to convert raw pulse data to breath rate; using machine learning algorithms to check for overdose; using board layout tools (Altium 18) to fabricate custom PCBs. Mechanical: Designing an injection mechanism that can administer antidotes to prevent death from slowdown of breathing (Solidworks).

ESE 111 Final Project

Ad-hoc network of smart lifejackets to coordinate and optimize rescue efforts.

Final project for freshman electrical engineering class. Worked with a teammate. Used Arduino to collect vital sign and location data from lifejacket wearers, and XBee radios to communicate data between lifejackets. See full details on Devpost.

ESE 112

Recruited to help design a new freshman electromagnetics class, wrote labs and designed experiments.

Co-wrote Labs 1-6 for the class and designed the experiments within them, available at https://ese112.seas.upenn.edu/labs/. Videos for some of the experiments are available here. The labs were designed to introduce basic concepts in electromagnetics in an intuitive way--the class was a success in its first semester and many other departments in the engineering school are recommending the class to their students. Watch the video for one of the experiments from Lab 3 on the right--in it, we use a ferrite to change the inductance of an inductor and trigger an alarm.
